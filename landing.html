Labor Force Participation Rate vs. Unemployment Rate

The unemployment rate is often cited as a prominent indicator of U.S. economic performance, but the metric excludes a key consideration—those that are not actively seeking employment. Why is it important to consider those not currently looking for a job? Simply put, those individuals not looking to work are missed opportunities for the U.S. work force, productivity, and economic growth—and reasons for non-participation include factors like discouragement after not finding work after a period of time, poor health, and unavailable work environments that allow a balance of family life. The latter reason has a particular impact on women in U.S. society.
